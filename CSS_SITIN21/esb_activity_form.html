Criteria ESB Microservices
Integration Style Central integration hub for routing and orchestration. Distributed service-to-service integration.
Routing and Control Centralized, easier to manage in one place. Spread across many services and APIs.
Deployment Complexity Lower for existing school systems. Higher due to many independently deployed services.
Scalability Moderate, may bottleneck at the bus in heavy load. High, each service scales independently.
Monitoring Easier troubleshooting from one integration point. Needs distributed tracing and more observability tools.
Best Fit for Scenario Strong fit for school sit-in and lab systems. Better for very large, fast-growing platforms.
Conclusion: ESB is better for this school scenario because it is simpler to operate and keeps modules coordinated. Microservices become better when the platform grows into a much larger, high-traffic environment.
